Introduction to Korean Adverbs

Adverbs in Korean are essential for adding depth and nuance to your expressions. They modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs, helping you communicate more effectively and naturally.

In this presentation, we'll explore:

  • Various types of Korean adverbs
  • How to form adverbs from adjectives
  • Using adverbs in Formal Polite and Informal Polite speech
  • Proper placement of adverbs in sentences
  • Special cases and common expressions

Types of Adverbs

A. Definition

Adverbs in Korean (부사 - busa) are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They provide information about how, when, where, or to what extent an action is performed.

B. Categories of Adverbs

  • Time adverbs (시간 부사 - sigan busa): Indicate when an action occurs
  • Manner adverbs (방법 부사 - bangbeop busa): Describe how an action is performed
  • Place adverbs (장소 부사 - jangso busa): Specify where an action takes place
  • Degree adverbs (정도 부사 - jeongdo busa): Express the intensity or extent of an action
  • Frequency adverbs (빈도 부사 - bindo busa): Show how often an action occurs

C. Examples

시간 (Time): 지금 (jigeum) - now, 어제 (eoje) - yesterday, 내일 (naeil) - tomorrow

방법 (Manner): 빨리 (ppalli) - quickly, 조용히 (joyonghi) - quietly, 열심히 (yeolsimhi) - diligently

장소 (Place): 여기 (yeogi) - here, 저기 (jeogi) - there, 어디 (eodi) - where

정도 (Degree): 매우 (maeu) - very, 너무 (neomu) - too much, 거의 (geoui) - almost

빈도 (Frequency): 자주 (jaju) - often, 가끔 (gakkeum) - sometimes, 항상 (hangsang) - always

Forming Adverbs from Adjectives

A. General Rules

While Korean adverbs don't conjugate based on tense or formality, many are derived from adjectives. This process can be considered a type of "conjugation" or transformation.

B. Common Patterns

1. For most adjectives, add -게 (-ge) to the stem:

  • 빠르다 (ppareuda - fast) → 빠르게 (ppareuge - quickly)
  • 느리다 (neurida - slow) → 느리게 (neurige - slowly)

2. For adjectives ending in 하다 (-hada), replace 하다 with 히 (-hi):

  • 조용하다 (joyonghada - quiet) → 조용히 (joyonghi - quietly)
  • 정확하다 (jeonghwakhada - accurate) → 정확히 (jeonghwakhi - accurately)

C. Special Cases

Some adverbs have irregular forms:

  • 좋다 (jota - good) → 잘 (jal - well)
  • 나쁘다 (nappuda - bad) → 나쁘게 (nappuge - badly) or 못 (mot - poorly)
  • 많다 (manta - many) → 많이 (mani - much, a lot)

Using Adverbs in Sentences

A. Adverb Placement

In Korean, adverbs typically precede the word they modify. The basic structure for using adverbs in Korean sentences is:

[Subject] + [Adverb] + [Object] + [Verb]

However, adverbs can also be placed at the beginning of a sentence for emphasis.

B. Examples in Different Speech Levels

1. Formal Polite (합쇼체 - hapsyo-che):

저는 천천히 책을 읽습니다. (Jeoneun cheoncheonhi chaegeul ilgseumnida.)

I slowly read the book.

2. Informal Polite (해요체 - haeyo-che):

그는 자주 운동해요. (Geuneun jaju undonghaeyo.)

He exercises often.

3. Adverb at the beginning (emphasis):

빨리 우리가 학교에 가요. (Ppalli uriga hakgyoe gayo.)

Quickly, we go to school.

Adverbs with Different Verb Tenses

A. Present Tense

나는 항상 아침을 먹어요. (Naneun hangsang achimeul meogeoyo.)

I always eat breakfast.

B. Past Tense

어제 늦게 잤어요. (Eoje neutge jasseoyo.)

I slept late yesterday.

C. Future Tense

내일 일찍 일어날 거예요. (Naeil iljjik ireonnal geoyeyo.)

I will wake up early tomorrow.

Tip: Adverb Consistency

Remember that adverbs in Korean do not change form based on the tense of the verb. The same adverb form is used regardless of whether the action is in the past, present, or future.

Common Adverbs and Their Usage

A. Frequency Adverbs

Korean Romanization English Example
항상 hangsang always 나는 항상 아침을 먹어요. (I always eat breakfast.)
자주 jaju often 그는 자주 운동해요. (He exercises often.)
가끔 gakkeum sometimes 저는 가끔 영화를 봐요. (I sometimes watch movies.)
거의 안 geoui an rarely 그녀는 거의 안 술을 마셔요. (She rarely drinks alcohol.)

B. Degree Adverbs

Korean Romanization English Example
매우 maeu very 이 음식은 매우 맛있어요. (This food is very delicious.)
아주 aju very 그 영화는 아주 재미있었어요. (That movie was very interesting.)
조금 jogeum a little 저는 조금 피곤해요. (I'm a little tired.)
너무 neomu too (much) 이 가방은 너무 비싸요. (This bag is too expensive.)
